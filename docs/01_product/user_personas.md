# User Personas

## Persona 1 — The Pragmatic Home Cook

**Name:** Maya, 34  
**Occupation:** Software developer, works from home  
**Tech comfort:** High

**Context:**  
Maya cooks most of her meals but spends too long deciding what to make each week. She's tried meal kit services but resents the cost and packaging. She wants a system that suggests meals she'd actually cook, respects that she doesn't eat red meat, and tells her what to buy — without requiring a login or monthly fee.

**Goals:**
- Have a meal plan ready by Sunday evening for the week ahead
- Avoid repeating the same 5 meals every week
- Get a clean shopping list she can screenshot and take to the supermarket

**Frustrations:**
- Apps that require an account just to get started
- Nutritional data that feels like pseudoscience
- Suggestions that ignore her dietary preferences

**How Suppergeist helps:** Local AI generates a varied plan tuned to her preferences; shopping list is ready in one click.

## Persona 2 — The Casual User (Secondary)

**Name:** Tom, 28  
**Occupation:** Teacher  
**Tech comfort:** Medium

**Context:**  
Tom doesn't cook adventurously but wants to eat better. He uses whatever's on his laptop. He's not interested in how the app works internally — he just wants meal suggestions that aren't intimidating to cook.

**Goals:**
- Quick meal ideas that use common ingredients
- A shopping list that doesn't assume he has a spice rack
- Simple interface with no learning curve

**Frustrations:**
- Apps with too many options and settings
- Suggestions that assume culinary expertise
- Anything that makes him create an account

**How Suppergeist helps:** Single-action plan generation; preferences system lets him constrain complexity over time.
